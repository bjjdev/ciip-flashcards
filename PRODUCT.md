# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo learner — an MRI Technologies employee studying independently for the ABII CIIP (Certified Imaging Informatics Professional) certification exam. Uses the app in focused, intentional study sessions. High-stakes context: the exam is a professional credential, not a hobby. The user is technically literate, comfortable with keyboard shortcuts, and wants a tool that respects their time and intelligence.

## Product Purpose

Spaced repetition flashcard app for ABII CIIP exam prep. 159 cards across 10 exam domains, weighted by exam score. SM-2 algorithm schedules cards based on self-rated recall. Three views: domain dashboard (home), study session, and stats/analytics. Goal: pass the exam by building real retention, not false confidence.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Purposeful. Understated.

The app should feel like a serious professional instrument — not a consumer wellness product, not a startup's marketing page. The emotional tone is calm confidence: you're prepared, you know the material, the tool is just doing its job.

## Anti-references

- **GitHub UI** — the current look. Too repository-viewer, too blue/grey neutral, no personality.
- **Generic SaaS** — no teal-and-white startup aesthetic, no rounded pill buttons everywhere, no hero-metric dashboards.
- **Flashy / gradient-heavy** — no neon gradients, glassmorphism, purple-pink AI aesthetic, or glowing blobs.
- **Gamified learning apps** — no Duolingo energy: no cartoon mascots, no confetti overload, no streaks as the hero metric.

## Design Principles

1. **Precision over decoration** — every element earns its space. If it doesn't serve the study session or the learner's state, it doesn't exist.
2. **Power-user feel** — feels fast, keyboard-first, capable. The interface respects the user's intelligence and doesn't over-explain.
3. **Earned feedback** — the UI acknowledges progress clearly but doesn't celebrate shallow activity. Retention matters; sessions completed is secondary.
4. **Dark without drama** — dark theme is correct for focused study, but it should have warmth and intentionality, not just inverted-light-theme grey.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Keyboard navigation is already core to the app (Space/Arrow/1-2-3 keys). Ensure sufficient contrast on all text against dark backgrounds, especially muted-tone labels.
